Light & Visibility

0 of 5 done

Light is what separates a bright, airy listing from one buyers scroll past.

Lit rooms photograph larger, warmer, and more inviting. Every bulb counts.

Natural light is free staging — and it makes every room feel bigger on camera.

Mixed color temps read as neglect on camera.

A black screen disappears. A frozen one steals the frame.

Spinning blades blur every shot.

Clear the Space

0 of 5 done

You're not hiding your life — you're making room for theirs. Clear surfaces sell square footage.

The kitchen makes or breaks a listing. Bare counters read as spacious, modern, move-in ready.

One product on the ledge ruins the shot. Think boutique hotel: nothing visible, nothing personal.

If you can see it, the camera can. Buyers need to picture their life here, not yours.

Bare floors photograph larger and read cleaner.

Garage, kennel, or offsite. Beds, bowls, toys too.

The 15-Minute Final Sweep.

  • All lights on. Every ceiling fan off
  • Every vehicle off the driveway and street
  • Toilet lids closed in every bathroom
  • Floors vacuumed or swept
  • Pets relocated — beds, bowls, toys gone
  • Golden rule: if it wouldn't be in a luxury hotel room, it shouldn't be here
