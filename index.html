90% of Staging Works. The Other 10% Is This.

Stagers and photographers share the same goal — make the home look its absolute best. There's about a 90% overlap in what works for both. The other 10% is where photography requires a completely different approach.

What feels warm and inviting in person often reads as clutter on camera. Decorative touches that stagers love are exactly what the lens turns into visual noise. The human eye and a wide-angle lens are not the same thing.

Great photos bring more buyers through the door. But the camera needs to stay focused on the property — not your appliances, plants, or personal decor. Work through the checklist below and your home will photograph spacious, clean, and at its absolute best.

Lit rooms photograph 40% larger and feel warm + inviting. Every bulb counts.

The 15-Minute Final Sweep.

  • All lights on. Every ceiling fan off
  • Every vehicle off the driveway and street
  • Toilet lids closed in every bathroom
  • Floors vacuumed or swept
  • Pets relocated — beds, bowls, toys gone
  • Golden rule: if it wouldn't be in a luxury hotel room, it shouldn't be here
